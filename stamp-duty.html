How residential SDLT works after 1 April 2025

For residential property in England and Northern Ireland, standard SDLT starts at £125,000. The main bands are 0% up to £125,000, 2% on the next £125,000, 5% on the portion from £250,001 to £925,000, 10% on the portion from £925,001 to £1.5 million and 12% above that. This page uses those current residential rates rather than the temporary thresholds that ended on 31 March 2025.

If you are a first-time buyer, relief currently applies only if the property price is £500,000 or less. In that case, there is no SDLT up to £300,000, and 5% applies on the portion from £300,001 to £500,000. If the price is above £500,000, the first-time buyer relief does not apply and the standard residential bands are used instead.

Additional properties now use higher rates than before

One of the biggest reasons older stamp duty pages become misleading is that the additional dwelling surcharge changed. For purchases completing on or after 1 April 2025, the higher residential rates for additional properties are now 5%, 7%, 10%, 15% and 17%. That means a simple “plus 3%” shortcut is no longer correct for current transactions.

What this page is good for

This calculator is useful when you want a fast planning estimate for a normal residential purchase in England or Northern Ireland. It is especially helpful for comparing buyer types, checking whether first-time buyer relief still helps at a given price, and seeing roughly how much the higher rates add for a second home or buy-to-let purchase.

When this page is only a rough guide

Property tax is full of exceptions. You should treat this result as a planning estimate if you are dealing with trusts, company purchases, leases with rental SDLT, shared ownership, mixed-use property, multiple dwellings, replacement main residence refunds, or any transaction where reliefs and ownership history are complicated.

Do first-time buyers still get a stamp duty discount?

Yes, but the current relief is narrower than the temporary rules many people still remember. At the moment there is no SDLT up to £300,000, then 5% on the portion from £300,001 to £500,000, and no first-time buyer relief at all above £500,000.

Do I pay stamp duty on an additional property?

Usually yes, if buying the new property means you will own more than one residential property at the end of the day of completion and no exception applies. From 1 April 2025 the higher rates are 5%, 7%, 10%, 15% and 17% across the main residential bands.

When does SDLT have to be filed and paid?

You must send an SDLT return to HMRC and pay the tax within 14 days of completion. In many cases your solicitor or conveyancer will do this for you and collect the amount as part of completion funds.
