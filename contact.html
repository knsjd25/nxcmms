What to include in a helpful message

The clearer the message, the easier it is to act on it. If you are reporting a problem, details are more useful than urgency.

  • The page name or URL. This helps narrow the issue quickly.
  • What you expected and what actually happened. A short comparison saves time.
  • Your device or browser if relevant. Some issues only appear in certain environments.
  • A screenshot if it helps explain the problem. Visual context is often the fastest way to understand a bug.

Before sending sensitive material

Some pages on the site are local-only tools, while others may rely on remote services. Email is not the right place for secrets, passwords, or highly sensitive personal data unless you have a good reason and understand the risk.

Keep contact messages focused on the issue itself. Do not send credentials, payment details, or anything you would not want sitting in plain email.
