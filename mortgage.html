How this mortgage calculator works

This page estimates monthly repayments using a standard amortisation formula for repayment mortgages, or monthly interest for interest-only mortgages. It also shows the total interest over the full term and calculates loan-to-value, which is the mortgage amount divided by the property value.

What LTV means

LTV stands for loan-to-value. A mortgage of £240,000 on a property worth £300,000 gives an 80% LTV. Lower LTVs often unlock better rates, while higher LTVs can mean fewer products and higher costs.

Does this calculator include Stamp Duty?

Yes. It includes an estimate of residential SDLT for England and Northern Ireland. It supports main residence purchases, first-time buyer relief, the higher rates for additional properties, and the optional 2% non-UK resident surcharge.

What is the difference between repayment and interest-only?

Repayment mortgages gradually pay down the balance as well as the interest, so the loan reaches zero by the end of the term if payments are made as planned. Interest-only mortgages estimate the monthly interest charge, but the original loan balance still needs repaying separately at the end.

Why test higher rates?

Rate stress testing gives a quick view of what your budget might look like if your fixed deal ends or rates move higher. It is not a lender affordability check, but it is useful for planning.
