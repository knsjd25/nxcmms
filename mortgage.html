What is LTV (Loan-to-Value)?

LTV (Loan-to-Value) is a critical metric. It represents the ratio of the loan amount compared to the total value of the property. For example, if you buy a £300,000 house and put down a deposit of £60,000, you need a mortgage of £240,000. Your LTV is calculated as: (£240,000 / £300,000) * 100 = 80%. A lower LTV grants you access to cheaper, more competitive interest rates.
