Understanding VAT in the UK (2026)

Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax placed on a product whenever value is added at each stage of the supply chain, from production to the point of sale. Using an online VAT calculator helps businesses and consumers quickly find the price before or after tax.

VAT Type Rate Typical Examples
Standard Rate 20% Most goods and services
Reduced Rate 5% Home energy, children's car seats
Zero Rate 0% Most food, children's clothes, books

How to Calculate VAT Manually?

To add VAT to an amount, multiply the net amount by 1.20 (for 20% VAT). To remove VAT and find the original price, divide the gross amount by 1.20.
