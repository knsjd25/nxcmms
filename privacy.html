Privacy Policy

How Mini-Tools handles browser processing, uploads, and basic site data.

Different pages on this site work in different ways. Some run locally in the browser. Some need a remote service. This policy explains those differences in plain language.

This page is a general description of how the site works and how data may be handled on different parts of Mini-Tools. It should be read together with the actual behavior of each tool page.

1. Browser-based tools

Many pages on Mini-Tools are designed to run entirely in the browser. In those cases, the main processing happens on your device rather than on a remote application server.

Examples can include formatting text, generating a QR code, resizing an image locally, validating JSON, or comparing two blocks of text. When a tool works this way, the site should describe that clearly on the page itself.

  • Local processing means the task happens in the browser. It does not mean the entire website is disconnected from the internet.
  • The page may still load libraries, fonts, analytics, or other static assets. Those are separate from the core tool action itself.
  • The exact behavior can vary by page. Users should rely on the page description and actual function of the tool.

2. Pages that use remote services

Some pages cannot do their main job without sending data to a remote service. A good example is an upload page that creates a shareable hosted file link. In that case, the file has to leave the browser so the service can store it and return a URL.

When a page depends on a remote service, Mini-Tools aims to explain that directly instead of presenting it as if it were a local-only tool.

  • Upload pages may send files to a remote endpoint.
  • Retention or deletion settings may be available on those pages.
  • Hosted results exist outside the browser session.

3. Analytics and basic website data

Mini-Tools uses Google Analytics on site pages to understand broad usage patterns, such as which pages are visited and how visitors move around the site. This is different from the content a browser-based tool may process locally.

Analytics can involve cookies or similar measurement technologies from Google. For more information about how Google handles this type of data, refer to Google’s own policies and controls.

4. Advertising

Some parts of the site may include advertising, while trust pages such as About, Contact, and Privacy may be kept free of advertising. When advertising is present on a page, third-party ad services may use cookies or similar technologies in line with their own policies.

5. Local storage and browser storage

Some pages may use browser storage, such as localStorage, to remember simple preferences or keep a tool state convenient between visits. This kind of storage stays in the browser unless the page itself explicitly sends something elsewhere.

6. Links to third-party services

The site may link to outside services, documentation, or platforms. If you follow those links, their own privacy terms and site behavior apply. Mini-Tools does not control third-party websites.

7. Contact messages

If you contact the site by email, the information you send becomes part of that email conversation. For that reason, do not include passwords, payment details, or highly sensitive information unless you understand the risk of email.

8. Changes to this policy

As the site grows and more tools are added or rewritten, privacy wording may be updated to better match how pages actually work. The goal is to keep this policy aligned with the real behavior of the site rather than make broader claims than necessary.
