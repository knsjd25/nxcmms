What this salary calculator includes

This page is built to be more useful to users and easier for search engines to trust. Instead of pretending to be an exact payroll system, it clearly states its scope: an annualised estimate for employee salary income in the 2026/27 tax year.

The calculator includes the standard personal allowance, Income Tax, employee National Insurance, salary sacrifice pension, Student Loan Plans 1, 2, 4 and 5, plus an optional Postgraduate Loan deduction.

What it does not include

  • Custom tax codes, company benefits or benefits in kind
  • Marriage Allowance, Blind Person’s Allowance or other special reliefs
  • Payroll-specific HMRC rounding and irregular pay treatment
  • Dividend income, self-employed tax, child benefit charge or savings/dividend tax

National Insurance and loan assumptions

Employee National Insurance is estimated using the 2026/27 Class 1 employee thresholds and rates. The page also includes Student Loan and Postgraduate Loan planning estimates, which helps the result feel closer to a real payslip than a basic tax-only calculator.

Does this calculator work for Scotland?

Yes. Switch the region to Scotland and the page uses Scottish 2026/27 non-savings income tax bands. England, Wales and Northern Ireland use the rest-of-UK salary bands.

Why does the page say estimate instead of exact?

Because that is more honest and more useful. Employer payroll can differ because of tax code changes, pay frequency rounding, benefits, pension setup and other payroll details.

How is pension handled here?

This version treats pension as salary sacrifice. That means the pension amount is removed before Income Tax, National Insurance and loan deductions are estimated.
