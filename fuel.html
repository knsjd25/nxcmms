How this UK fuel calculator works

This page is designed around the way UK drivers usually think about fuel. Distances are entered in miles, vehicle efficiency is entered in imperial MPG, and the pump price is entered in pence per litre. The calculator converts those measurements into a single journey estimate without making you switch between systems manually.

The calculation behind the result

The estimate works in three steps. First, distance is divided by MPG to estimate the number of imperial gallons used. Second, imperial gallons are converted into litres using the UK conversion factor of 4.54609. Third, litres are multiplied by the pump price in pence and then converted into pounds.

Formula: total cost = ((distance ÷ MPG) × 4.54609 × price per litre in pence) ÷ 100

Why this is useful

  • It is useful for comparing one-way and return trips before you travel.
  • It helps with informal cost sharing when several people are splitting petrol or diesel.
  • It shows litres and gallons together, which makes the estimate easier to sense-check.

Frequently asked questions

Does this calculator use a UK gallon or a US gallon?

It uses the UK imperial gallon. That matters because a UK gallon is larger than a US gallon, so using the wrong conversion changes the estimated litres and total cost.

Is this suitable for exact reimbursement claims?

No. This page is a planning estimate. Employer mileage rates, business reimbursement rules and real-world fuel consumption may all differ from the simple journey estimate shown here.

Can I use it for petrol and diesel?

Yes. The calculator only needs your actual pump price and your vehicle efficiency. It does not assume a specific fuel type.
